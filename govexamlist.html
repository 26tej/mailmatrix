    UPSC Civil Services Exam

    Eligibility: Graduation in any discipline

    The UPSC Civil Services Exam is conducted to recruit candidates for various civil services of the Government of India, including the Indian Administrative Service (IAS) and Indian Police Service (IPS).

    Exam Structure: The exam consists of three stages: Preliminary Exam, Main Exam, and Personality Test (Interview). The Prelims consists of two objective papers, while the Mains includes nine papers of descriptive type.

    Career Fields: Successful candidates can work in diverse roles within the government, including administrative, police, revenue services, and foreign services, contributing to governance and public administration.

    SSC Combined Graduate Level (CGL)

    Eligibility: Graduation in any discipline

    The SSC CGL exam is held for selecting candidates for various posts in ministries, departments, and organizations of the Government of India.

    Exam Structure: The CGL exam comprises four tiers: Tier I (Computer-Based Examination), Tier II (Descriptive Examination), Tier III (Skill Test), and Tier IV (Computer Proficiency Test or Document Verification).

    Career Fields: Candidates can be placed in roles such as Assistant Audit Officer, Inspector, and various administrative positions within government departments.

    IBPS PO Exam

    Eligibility: Graduation in any discipline

    The IBPS PO Exam is conducted for the recruitment of Probationary Officers in various public sector banks across India.

    Exam Structure: The exam consists of three phases: Preliminary Exam, Main Exam, and Interview. The Preliminary exam has objective questions, while the Main exam includes both objective and descriptive questions.

    Career Fields: Selected candidates work as Probationary Officers and can rise to managerial positions in banks, handling customer services, loans, and operations.

    Railway Recruitment Board (RRB) NTPC

    Eligibility: 10th/12th/Graduation (depends on the post)

    The RRB NTPC exam is conducted to recruit candidates for various posts in Indian Railways, including Non-Technical Popular Categories.

    Exam Structure: The selection process includes a Computer Based Test (CBT), followed by a Skill Test (where applicable), and Document Verification.

    Career Fields: Successful candidates may be placed in roles such as Clerk, Ticket Collector, and in various operational and administrative positions within the Indian Railways.

    Indian Navy Recruitment Exam

    Eligibility: 10th/12th/Graduation (depends on the post)

    The Indian Navy conducts recruitment exams for various positions, including sailors and officers, in the naval service.

    Exam Structure: The recruitment process typically includes a written examination, physical fitness test, and medical examination. For officer roles, an interview is also conducted.

    Career Fields: Candidates can serve in various capacities within the Navy, such as engineering, logistics, and naval architecture, playing vital roles in maritime defense.
